As far back as I can remember...

I was curious about how things worked. It started with Legos and K'nex and going to work with my father watching him doing cool stuff. Growing up I would constantly frustrate my mother by deconstructing old toys and appliances to see how they worked. It was rare that I ever put them back together again, but I would take the motor from the coffee grinder, the battery from my r/c car, (and with the help of a lot of duct tape and legos) slap together a boat for the bathtub.

This went on throughout my childhood. If I didn't have something, I made it.

At the time I didn't know how lucky I was, but I soaked it all up. I was hooked. No longer looking for a music university, I knew engineering was my home. The opportunity that I had in HS solidified my passion for engineering and showed me that building complex things are fun and totally possible.
